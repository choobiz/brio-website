From aging property to modern duplex: a North Vancouver transformation done right.

Updated February 19, 2025

**Quick Summary:**  
At BRIO Construction, we believe homes should evolve with the people who live in them. In this blog, we take you behind the scenes of how we transformed a run-down property into a beautifully functional **multi-generational home** designed for comfort, privacy, and togetherness—three generations under one roof.

## Why Multi-Generational Homes Are on the Rise

Family dynamics are shifting—and so is the way we live. More families are coming together across generations to share living spaces, for reasons ranging from affordability and caregiving to stronger family bonds.

According to the [Pew Research Center](https://www.pewresearch.org/), over 64 million North Americans were living in multi-generational households as of 2016. That number is growing rapidly, particularly in urban markets where housing costs continue to climb.

We’re seeing this demand locally too. From [Vancouver](/vancouver) to [North Vancouver](/north-vancouver) and [Burnaby](/burnaby), more families are asking us for home designs that accommodate grandparents, adult children, and young kids—all under one roof.

## The Vision: A Home That Brings Everyone Together—Comfortably

Our client approached us with a big idea: take a dilapidated tear-down and turn it into a spacious, welcoming **multi-generational home**.  
The old house had good bones but poor layout, limited space, and outdated everything. Instead of building just another modern single-family residence, the family wanted a space where everyone could thrive.

This was more than a renovation. It was a reimagining.

## Planning a Home for Multiple Generations

The project started with deep planning and discovery. During our pre-construction & development process, we asked questions like:

-   How many people will live here—and for how long?
    
-   How much privacy does each generation need?
    
-   What kind of shared spaces will support connection?
    

From those insights, we drafted a plan that would blend independence and community seamlessly.

### 🏠 Key Design Features:

-   **Private Living Quarters:** Each generation had its own wing with bedrooms, bathrooms, and even kitchenettes for privacy and independence.
    
-   **Connected Common Areas:** The family shares a central kitchen, living room, and dining area to enjoy meals and time together.
    
-   **Accessibility Considerations:** We added features like step-free entrances, wider hallways, and main-floor bedrooms to support aging parents.
    
-   **Soundproofing Between Suites:** Because quiet is golden when living under one roof.
    

## Navigating Zoning and Permits

One of the trickiest parts of building a **multi-generational home** is dealing with local zoning rules, which often weren’t designed for this kind of setup.

Thanks to our experience building in areas like [Coquitlam](/coquitlam) and [Richmond](/richmond), we knew how to:

-   Apply for the right variances
    
-   Work with inspectors on layout and suite requirements
    
-   Maximize usable square footage while staying within code
    

We also ensured energy efficiency compliance and incorporated high-performance HVAC and insulation systems to keep utility bills low—even with a full house.

## Bringing the Vision to Life

Once the plans and permits were approved, we moved quickly through demo and framing, building a two-storey, multi-wing layout with family in mind.

Every choice was intentional:

-   A modern but warm exterior to blend with the neighborhood
    
-   Durable, low-maintenance materials to keep the home resilient for years
    
-   Smart tech systems for security, climate control, and lighting
    

The final result? A 3-generation **multi-generational home** that balances form and function.
