Welcome to the new home of the **BRIO Construction blog**. We've rebuilt the entire reading experience from scratch — faster pages, a cleaner layout, and tools that make our renovation guides genuinely useful instead of just SEO filler.

If you've read our older posts, you'll notice a few things have changed.

## What's new

The new template was designed around three things our readers told us mattered most: **clarity, scannability, and trust**. Every section now has a clear purpose, callouts highlight the things you actually need to act on, and we've added before/after comparisons for projects where seeing the difference is half the story.

<CalloutCard tone="key" title="The short version">
We rebuilt the blog to load faster, read more easily, and share better. Same renovation expertise — better packaging.
</CalloutCard>

### A reading experience that respects your time

Long-form construction guides are useful, but they need to stay *navigable*. On desktop you'll see a sticky table of contents on the left — click any heading to jump straight to that section. On mobile, the same TOC is one tap away at the top of every article. A reading-progress bar at the top shows you how far through the article you are.

### Real expertise, attributed to real people

Every post now has a named author with their actual role at BRIO — not a generic house byline. That matters because when you're reading about Vancouver basement-suite legalization, it's worth knowing whether the writer has actually pulled that permit before.

## What you can expect from us going forward

We're publishing new guides on three tracks:

1. **Permits and process** — practical, specific, "do this in this order" walkthroughs for Vancouver, North Van, Richmond, and the rest of Metro Vancouver.
2. **Design and material decisions** — what we'd actually do (and why) on real budgets in 2026.
3. **What things really cost** — honest breakdowns of the line items most contractors won't show you until quote day.

<PullQuote attribution="Built by BRIO">
The construction industry is built on jargon and opacity. Our blog is built on the opposite of that.
</PullQuote>

## A note on what we're not doing

We're not chasing trends or writing thinly-sourced "10 things you need to know" listicles. If a topic doesn't help you make a real decision about a real project, we won't write about it. That's it — that's the whole content strategy.

If there's something specific you'd like us to write about, **reply to any of our newsletters** or drop us a note via the form below. We read every message.
