**Quick Summary:**  
In Vancouver’s highly competitive real estate market, smart home renovations can significantly boost your home’s value—whether you’re planning to sell or want to invest in long-term equity. In this blog, we’ll explore the top **home renovations that increase property value**, with data-backed insights, expert advice, and practical tips to help you get the best return on your investment.

## Kitchen Remodels: The Heart of the Home (and Your ROI)

It’s no surprise that kitchen remodels consistently top the list of value-boosting renovations.  
In Vancouver, a well-designed kitchen renovation can recoup **75% to 100%** of its cost, especially when it includes:

-   New countertops (stone or quartz)
    
-   Updated cabinetry
    
-   Modern, energy-efficient appliances
    
-   Improved lighting and layout

## 🚿 2. Bathroom Remodels (Midrange): ROI up to **74.3%**

Bathrooms remain a hot-ticket item, and for good reason. A midrange bathroom remodel now yields **up to 74.3%** return, especially when it focuses on maximizing space and improving comfort.

### 🚰 Upgrade Ideas That Sell:

-   Modern vanities with integrated lighting
    
-   Walk-in showers with glass doors
    
-   Dual-flush or low-flow toilets
    
-   Durable, stylish tile work

## ➕ 3. Home Additions: ROI up to **84.0%**

Adding square footage—whether it’s a bedroom, home office, or full suite—can significantly increase your home’s value. In 2024, **a new primary suite addition delivers up to 84.0% ROI**, particularly when it includes an ensuite bath.

### 🧱 Types of Additions with Strong Returns:

-   Extra bedroom or guest suite
    
-   Second-story addition or bump-out
    
-   Basement conversion or legal suite
    
-   Open-concept family room extension
    

🏡 More space = more value in places like [Coquitlam](/coquitlam) and [New Westminster](/new-westminster).

👉 Explore [BRIO’s Home Addition services](/home-additions-extensions).

## 🏗️ 4. Open-Concept Layouts: ROI up to **62.1%**

Open floor plans continue to be a buyer favorite—especially post-pandemic when people are spending more time at home. Strategic wall removals and space reconfigurations can return **62.1% or more**, depending on the scope.

### 🏠 Design Features That Work:

-   Remove barriers between kitchen and living spaces
    
-   Improve lighting with skylights or large windows
    
-   Use structural beams to open up floor space while maintaining integrity

## 🌿 5. Energy-Efficient Upgrades: ROI + Cost Savings

While the ROI on things like insulation or new windows can range from **52% to 69%**, the **long-term savings** on utilities and improved comfort make them a smart move. Plus, they’re aligned with BC’s [Step Code for Energy Efficiency](https://energystepcode.ca/).

### 🌎 Green Improvements That Count:

-   Triple-pane windows
    
-   Heat pump HVAC systems
    
-   Solar-ready roofing
    
-   Smart thermostats and lighting systems
    

♻️ Sustainability is becoming a major value-add, especially for younger homebuyers.

## 💰 What This Means for Vancouver Homeowners

Vancouver homes come with high price tags—so every renovation should be a strategic one. Based on the 2024 numbers, here are a few takeaways:

✔️ **Midrange projects** offer better ROI than luxury ones  
✔️ **Function-first designs** are outperforming flashy finishes  
✔️ **Curb appeal and efficiency** upgrades remain underrated winners
